Title: The Machine Learning Engineering Lifecycle
Project: Climate-Socio Dynamics AI

1. THE GOAL
We are not just "predicting the weather" (like will it rain tomorrow?).
We are modeling "Climate Dynamics." We want to know:
"If humanity changes its behavior (GDP, CO2), how does the Earth react over 50 years?"

2. THE ML WORKFLOW (The "Standard" Process)
Every professional AI project follows this pipeline. We did the exact same thing:

Step A: Data Ingestion & Cleaning (Phase 1)
   - Real-world data is messy.
   - Different timestamps (Yearly GDP vs Daily Weather).
   - "Garbage In, Garbage Out": If we don't fix the data, the AI learns nothing.

Step B: Feature Engineering & Selection (Phase 1)
   - We have hundreds of variables. Which ones actually matter?
   - We used statistical tests (Granger Causality) to scientifically prove that
     CO2 changes *cause* Temperature changes before we allowed the AI to use that data.

Step C: Model Architecture (Phase 2)
   - We selected an LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) Network.
   - Why? Because climate has "Memory." The temperature today depends on
     the accumulated heat from the last 10 years, not just yesterday.
     Standard formulas cannot capture this "Memory"; LSTMs can.

Step D: Training & Backtesting (Phase 2)
   - Training: We show the AI the years 1974-2014. It guesses, we correct it, it learns.
   - Backtesting: We hide the years 2014-2024. We ask the AI to predict them.
     We compare its guess to reality. This proves the model works.

Step E: Inference & Simulation (Phase 3)
   - Now that we trust the model, we use it to predict the future (2025-2075).
   - We build a "Scenario Engine" to ask "What if?" questions.

KEY TERMINOLOGY FOR PRESENTATION:
- "Multivariate": We use multiple variables (Temp, CO2, GDP) to predict one thing.
- "Time-Series": Data that is sequential (Time matters).
- "Supervised Learning": We trained the AI using historical data where we knew the correct answer.
